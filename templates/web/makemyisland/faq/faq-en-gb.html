Frequently Asked Questions

What is [% PROCESS 'site-name.html' -%]?
[% PROCESS 'site-name.html' -%] is a site to help people report to their local authority, view, or discuss local problems they’ve found by simply locating them on a map.
How do I get in touch with [% PROCESS 'site-name.html' -%]?
Here’s our contact page. Or simply send an SMS to 7592203.
What sort of problems should I report with [% PROCESS 'site-name.html' -%]?
Currently we're accepting reports about problems including:
  • Dumping waste in public areas (All islands)
  • Broken street lights (Laamu Atoll only)
  • Erosion of beaches (Laamu atoll only)
  • Damage to public property (Laamu Atoll only)
  • Graffiti (Laamu Atoll only)
  • Improper parking along the main road (Laamu Atoll only)
What isn’t [% PROCESS 'site-name.html' -%] for?
[% PROCESS 'site-name.html' -%] is not a way of getting in touch with[% c.cobrand.is_council ? 'the' : 'your' %] authority for all issues – please use [% PROCESS 'site-name.html' -%] only for problems such as the above. You will need to contact [% c.cobrand.is_council ? 'the' : 'your' %] authority directly for problems such as:
  • Anti-social behaviour
  • Any urgent or emergency problems
  • Noise pollution
  • Fires and smoke/smell pollution
  • Missing wheelie bins or recycling boxes
  • Missed rubbish collections
How do I use the site?
After entering a location, you'll see a map of that area. You can view problems already reported in that area, or report ones of your own simply by clicking on the map at the location of the problem.
How are the problems solved?
They are reported to the [% IF !c.cobrand.is_council %]relevant[% END %] authority by email. The authority can then resolve the problem the way they normally would. Alternatively, you can discuss the problem on the website with others, and then together lobby the authority to fix it, or fix it directly yourselves.
Is it free?
The site is free to use, yes. [% PROCESS 'site-name.html' -%] is maintained by the respective authorities who are mandated to address these issues. mySociety provide the FixMyStreet platform that's powering this site.
Can I use [% PROCESS 'site-name.html' -%] on my mobile?

The [% PROCESS 'site-name.html' -%] website will already work on your mobile phone, adapting to the size of your screen automatically. There will be [% PROCESS 'site-name.html' -%] apps for Android and iPhone.

Practical Questions

I’m from an authority, where do you send the reports?
You can either leave a test report or Contact us to find out where reports go at the moment. Also Contact us to ask us to update the address or addresses we use.
Will I remain anonymous after reporting an issue?
You can register yourself under any name on the website or use the mobile phone application to report issues under a pseudonym. However if you report through SMS, your mobile phone number will be sent to the authorities
I’m from an authority, can we have a [% PROCESS 'site-name.html' -%]?
Quite possibly - Contact the team to find out.
Do you remove silly or illegal content?
[% PROCESS 'site-name.html' -%] is not responsible for the content and accuracy of material submitted by its users. We reserve the right to edit or remove any problems or updates which we consider to be inappropriate upon being informed by a user of the site.
Why can’t I zoom out more on the reporting map?
We want to keep [% PROCESS 'site-name.html' -%] locally focused so you will not be able to report problems outside of the areas we have entered authorities for. The map on Your Reports will let you see all the reports you've made, wherever they are. If you're from an authority then the emailed version of the problem report may also contain the closest road or postcode to the pin on the map.
